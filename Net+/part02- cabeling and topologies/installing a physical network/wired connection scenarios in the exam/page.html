all the scenarios in the exam can be categorized into two main groups:

1- poor or slow connection: the main issues that will cause this are:

a- attenuation: where the signal weakens over distance and that's why we have very strict rules when it comes to distances in TIA.
b- jitter: is when packets get dropped and resent very frequently due to low bandwidth or other factors. the solution is buffering
c- incorrect cabling: cable mismatch causes connection to be slow or breaks frequently. solution is to ensure cable compatability


2- No connection at all:

a- bad ports: this happens when switch port goes dead and creating a loopback with the workstation itself
b- TX/RX reverse: mixing up cabling of crossover and straight through
c- bent pins: this could be an issue
d- open/short: this is when the problem is with the cabling especially with crimping of the rj-45
